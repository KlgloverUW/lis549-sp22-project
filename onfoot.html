Adventures on Foot

It is easier to take in Seattle's outdoors when you're not on the road. Here are some suggestions of places for wandering feet and eyes.

Art

Alexander Calder's eagle stays put along Elliott Bay in Olympic Sculpture Park.

Birds

A bald eagle was roosting in the cedar across from my studio apartment on Capitol Hill. Seagulls and crows dove at it until it left.

Get to know bird calls and characteristics at Cornell University Lab of Ornithology's All About Birds site. Search for birds by name and find tips on id-ing them.

Fish

Watch Coho, Chinook, and Sockeye salmon from the Pacific Ocean swim past the Ballard Locks to spawn in freshwater. June through October is the best time to see them.

Keep track of the endangered status of salmon and learn more about them on the federal government's National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration site. Look up which seafood can sustainably be eaten on the Monterey Bay Aquarium's Seafood Watch site.
